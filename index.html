Saturday Social
Social Media Agency - Buyer question: What does social media management cost?
GEO Page: What Social Media Management Really Costs in 2026
CONTENT Content Strategy The Playbook

The page answers the exact question buyers type before hiring an agency: "what does social media management cost?" It compares DIY, freelancer, and agency options so the reader can self-qualify before contacting Saturday Social.

  • Question-first architecture is the right call - cost comparison pages attract high-intent buyers who are already in the market. The page earns trust by being transparent rather than hiding pricing behind a form.
  • Three-option comparison matches the buyer's mental model - most prospects are already comparing DIY tools, freelancers, and agencies. The page enters that conversation directly.
  • Podcast + interactive format keeps the page alive - the page is not a static wall of text. It includes a podcast and interactive elements, which increases dwell time and signals quality to both Google and AI engines.
SEO SEO / GEO Structure My Contribution
  • H1 and subheadings map to AI answer formats - the H1 asks the question directly. H2s break down DIY vs freelancer vs agency. This is the exact structure LLMs pull from when generating answers.
  • Schema markup for FAQ and HowTo - structured data helps search engines understand the comparison format and increases the chance of rich snippets and AI citations.
  • Internal linking to service pages - the cost page links to Saturday Social's service and case study pages, passing authority and converting readers who want next steps.
  • Real numbers and sourcing - the page uses actual market data and rates, which builds trust and gives LLMs concrete facts to cite.
AI Where My AI Content System Fits Automation

AI search engines answer questions like "what does social media management cost" by pulling from pages that clearly compare options. My AI system structures the research, draft, and on-page schema so the content is citation-ready from the start.

  • Buyer question research at scale - the AI system identifies the cost-related questions prospects ask across the buyer journey, not just the head term.
  • Comparison format templates - the system generates the H2/H3 structure for DIY vs freelancer vs agency automatically, so every page follows the format LLMs prefer.
  • Fact-checking and sourcing prompts - every rate and statistic is flagged for verification before publication, keeping the page accurate and cite-worthy.
Galil Med Spa
Medical Aesthetics - Buyer question: What does a HIFU facial cost?
GEO Page: HIFU Facial Cost vs Microneedling vs Surgical Lift
CONTENT Content Strategy The Playbook

The page targets a high-intent medical aesthetics buyer who is comparing treatments and providers. By answering cost, downtime, and results upfront, it filters out price shoppers and attracts prospects who are ready to book a consultation.

  • Cost + comparison + provider is the right content triangle - buyers want to know price, how it compares to alternatives, and who they can trust. The page covers all three.
  • Medical credibility is non-negotiable - the page does not make unsupported claims. It explains how HIFU works, what it treats, and who is a good candidate, which protects the brand and helps the reader make an informed decision.
  • Local booking intent is built into the structure - the page is written for a specific Idaho market and includes clear next steps for consultation booking, not just education.
SEO SEO / GEO Structure My Contribution
  • H1 targets the comparison query - "What a HIFU Facial Costs (and How It Compares to Microneedling and a Surgical Lift)" captures both the cost intent and the comparison intent in one search-friendly headline.
  • Procedure comparison table - a clear table comparing HIFU, microneedling, and surgical lift gives both readers and AI engines a scannable fact base.
  • Medical FAQ schema - FAQ schema on safety, downtime, results, and candidacy helps the page appear in AI-generated answers and featured snippets.
  • Local service page links - internal links to the med spa's HIFU service and consultation pages convert educated readers into booked appointments.
AI Where My AI Content System Fits Automation

Medical aesthetics content has to be accurate and well-sourced. My AI system drafts the comparison framework, flags medical claims for human review, and structures the page so it is easy for both readers and LLMs to parse.

  • Competitor and alternative research - the AI system gathers how microneedling and surgical lift are described across authoritative sources, so the comparison is accurate and balanced.
  • Claim flagging for human verification - any statement about results, safety, or candidacy is tagged for review before publication, keeping the content compliant and trustworthy.
  • FAQ schema generation - the system extracts the most common questions from research and formats them into FAQ schema automatically.
Gardner Law
Estate Planning - Buyer question: Will or living trust in Idaho?
GEO Page: Will or Living Trust in Idaho
CONTENT Content Strategy The Playbook

The page answers a high-stakes question for Idaho families: which estate planning tool do they actually need? It replaces dense legal jargon with plain-language guidance and a clear recommendation framework.

  • Local specificity builds trust - Idaho probate laws and tax rules are different from other states. The page is written for Idaho residents, not a generic national audience.
  • Decision framework, not just definitions - the page does not just define wills and trusts. It tells the reader which one fits their situation based on assets, family structure, and goals.
  • Soft conversion to consultation - estate planning is a high-trust purchase. The page ends with a consultation CTA after educating the reader, which is the right sequence for this service.
SEO SEO / GEO Structure My Contribution
  • Local keyword targeting - the H1 includes "Idaho" and the page targets "will or trust Idaho," "Idaho living trust," and related local terms that indicate intent to hire a local attorney.
  • Clear H2 decision paths - H2s like "When a will is enough" and "When a trust makes sense" match the way AI engines structure comparison answers.
  • Legal disclaimer and authority signals - the page includes attorney bio links, disclaimers, and clear sourcing, which builds trust with both readers and AI systems.
  • Service and contact internal links - the page links to estate planning services, the attorney team, and the contact page to convert informed readers into consultations.
AI Where My AI Content System Fits Automation

Legal content has to be precise and jurisdiction-specific. My AI system researches Idaho-specific estate planning rules, drafts plain-language explanations, and flags anything that needs attorney review before the page goes live.

  • Jurisdiction-specific research - the AI system is prompted to prioritize Idaho probate, trust, and tax rules rather than generic US legal advice.
  • Plain-language rewriting - complex legal concepts are rewritten for a non-lawyer audience, then reviewed for accuracy before publication.
  • Attorney review checkpoints - the workflow flags sections that contain legal claims, ensuring a human expert reviews before the page is published.
Penta Electric
Electrical / Solar - Buyer question: Are solar panels worth it in Idaho in 2026?
GEO Page: Are Solar Panels Worth It in Idaho in 2026?
CONTENT Content Strategy The Playbook

The page addresses a buyer who is skeptical and researching a major purchase. It answers ROI, policy changes, and timing honestly, which builds authority and pre-qualifies the right leads for Penta Electric.

  • Honest framing beats hype - the title promises "an honest look after the rules changed." That tone matches how buyers research solar today and builds trust before the sales conversation.
  • 2026 policy update angle captures search demand - solar incentives and net metering rules change often. A page that addresses the latest rules ranks for time-sensitive searches and earns links from local news and forums.
  • ROI calculator or estimate framework - giving the reader a way to estimate payback period keeps them on the page and positions Penta Electric as the authority on Idaho solar math.
SEO SEO / GEO Structure My Contribution
  • Year-included H1 captures the update query - "Are solar panels worth it in Idaho in 2026?" ranks for both the evergreen question and the current-year policy search.
  • Policy and incentive section - a dedicated section on Idaho-specific incentives, tax credits, and utility rules gives LLMs concrete facts to cite and keeps the page fresh.
  • FAQ schema on payback, warranties, and installation - these are the exact questions buyers ask before contacting a solar installer. FAQ schema helps the page appear in AI answers.
  • Service page and quote CTA links - the page funnels educated readers into a free quote request, matching the high-intent solar buyer journey.
AI Where My AI Content System Fits Automation

Solar content goes stale fast because policies and incentives change. My AI system tracks rule changes, drafts updated sections, and flags every statistic and incentive for verification before the page is republished.

  • Policy change monitoring - the AI system is set up to detect changes in Idaho solar rules, net metering, and federal incentives so the page stays current.
  • ROI calculation framework - the system drafts payback examples based on current average system costs, electricity rates, and incentive values, then flags them for human review.
  • Update workflow - when rules change, the system identifies which sections need updates and suggests revised copy, reducing the time to keep the page accurate.
My SEO/GEO Philosophy

How I Think About
Content That Gets Cited.

Flodesk's goal is to turn AI search into a durable acquisition channel. Here's how I approach that work.

01

Citations Over Rankings

GEO is not traditional SEO with a new name. The goal is to be the source ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity pull from when someone asks about email marketing, automations, and selling online. Rankings matter, but citations are the new metric.

02

Prune Before You Grow

Thin and outdated content drags down a whole domain. I audit the portfolio, consolidate weak pages into stronger pieces, and keep an auditable record of every decision. Growth starts with removing what no longer serves the brand.

03

Templates That Scale

I build repeatable page formats for comparison guides, FAQ hubs, and how-to deep dives. Each template follows citation-ready structure, so the team can ship faster without losing quality or brand voice.

04

Structure for LLMs

Clear H1s, question-based H2s, schema markup, and concise answer blocks make content easier for LLMs to parse and cite. I ship structured data and semantic HTML with every page, not as an afterthought.

05

Test and Report

Every change runs against a baseline. I measure organic traffic, AI citations, share of voice, and qualified signups. Biweekly reports include what changed, why I think it changed, and what I'm testing next.

06

Brand Voice First

Flodesk's voice is simple, intuitive, and built for creators. Every AI-assisted draft is edited back to that voice before it ships. I use brand guidelines, personas, and competitive analysis as the input, not just the prompt.
